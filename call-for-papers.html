Presentation Formats

Paper Presentations

Each paper presentation will be allocated 20 minutes, followed by 5 minutes for audience discussion.

  • Empirical papers should include a clear rationale, theoretical framing, methodological details, key findings, and implications.
  • Conceptual papers should critically examine frameworks, policies, or emerging issues and propose new theoretical or practical insights.
  • Title: Maximum 20 words
  • Abstract length: Maximum 300 words
  • All accepted paper proposals will be considered for both oral and poster presentation formats. The final format will be confirmed in the official Acceptance Letter via email.

Poster Presentations

Posters provide a platform for sharing work-in-progress, pilot studies, or innovative practices. Presenters will discuss their work during a scheduled poster session.

  • Empirical papers must present a clear rationale, theoretical framing, methods, key findings, and implications, while conceptual papers should critique and advance frameworks, policies, or issues, with a title limited to 20 words and an abstract of up to 300 words.
  • Poster size: A1 (841 x 594 mm / 33.1 x 23.4 inches)

Symposia

Symposia feature three to four papers addressing a single, well-defined theme. Each symposium should include a moderator and, if possible, an independent discussant.

  • Total time: 90 minutes (including at least 15 minutes for discussion)
  • Title: Maximum 20 words
  • Proposal length: Maximum 800 words, including:
    • Overview of the symposium theme and goals
    • Titles and 150-word abstracts for each paper
    • Names and roles of the moderator/discussant
    • Planned time allocation
