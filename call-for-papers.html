Conference Theme

The Asian Association for Language Assessment (AALA) 2026 Annual Conference invites proposals exploring how humanistic perspectives can inform and transform contemporary assessment practices, policies, and technologies. As language assessment increasingly intersects with artificial intelligence, global mobility, and accountability pressures, it is vital to reaffirm the centrality of human agency, ethics, and equity in assessment design and use.

This year‚Äôs theme‚ÄîHumanistic Approach to Assessment, Standards, Innovation, and Accountability‚Äîencourages critical inquiry into how assessment can balance standardization with innovation, fairness with empathy, and accountability with trust.

AALA Student Awards

The AALA Best Student Paper Award and AALA Best Student Poster Award recognize outstanding research by graduate students.

  • All single-authored student submissions are automatically considered. For multi-authored submissions, all authors must be full-time students at the time of submission to be eligible for the two awards.
  • Applicants must indicate student status by ticking the corresponding statement in the Submission Form.
  • Proof of enrolment may be requested.
  • Winners will receive a certificate, waived registration for the main and pre-conference workshops, and an invitation to the conference banquet.
  • For details on previous recipients, visit AALA Award Winners Page.

Evaluation Criteria

All proposals will be double reviewed based on the following criteria:

  • Significance and contribution to language assessment research or practice
  • Relevance to the conference theme and sub-themes
  • Rigor of research design and theoretical grounding
  • Clarity of argumentation and implications
