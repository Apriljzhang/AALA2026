Venue Overview

The City University of Macau (CityU Macau), formerly known as the University of East Asia founded in 1981 and renamed in 2011, follows a strategic development outline of “Rename, Restructure, Transform, Upgrade” as it advances disciplines in digital humanities, arts, business, finance, emerging engineering, and social sciences to become a fully-fledged metropolitan university. As a leading education provider, the University shoulders the social responsibility of “Serving Macao, Integrating into Greater Bay Area” by nurturing high-caliber professionals aligned with its motto of “Virtue, Knowledge, Practice,” while sparing no effort to provide the intellectual and academic support necessary to promote local and regional development within the integrated Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area.

Entry Requirements & Visas

Most international travelers and participants are exempted from a visa or entry permit for Macao for a stay ranging from 7 to 90 days, depending on their nationality.

Transportation

Welcome to Macau! To ensure a smooth arrival for our international visitors, we have organized transportation options into three categories: By Air, By Land, and By Sea.

Below you will find detailed instructions and official links to help you reach the City University of Macau (Taipa Campus).

By Air

Macau International Airport (MFM)

Located directly in Taipa, this is the most convenient entry point for those with direct flights from major Asian cities.

  • To Campus: A 10-minute ride via Taxi, or take Public Bus AP1 or AP1X to Jardim Hoi Van.

Hong Kong International Airport (HKIA)

A primary hub for long-haul international travelers. You have two main options to transfer to Macau:

  • Direct Transit (No HK Immigration): Follow signs for "Mainland/Macau Connection" to the SkyPier Terminal. You can take a direct ferry to Macau or the "HKIA Direct" bus across the HZMB bridge. Your checked luggage can be transferred directly to Macau.
  • General Entry (Via HK): After clearing HK immigration and collecting your bags, take the B4 bus to the HZMB Hong Kong Port, then board the Golden Bus (HZMB Shuttle) to the Macau Port.

By Land

From Hong Kong (via HZMB)

The Hong Kong–Zhuhai–Macao Bridge (HZMB) is a 24-hour land link.

  • To Campus: Take the Golden Bus to the HZMB Macau Port. From there, take Bus 102X (~45 mins) and alight at CTM (氹仔電訊), then walk up the slope to the campus.

From Mainland China

Travelers arriving by High-Speed Rail will arrive at Zhuhai Railway Station, adjacent to the border crossings.

  • Gongbei / Border Gate (關閘): The most popular crossing. Take Bus 25B to Esparteiro/Regency (史伯泰/麗景灣站).
  • Qingmao Port (青茂): Pedestrian-only and open 24 hours (near Zhuhai Station). Take Bus 25B.
  • Hengqin Port (橫琴): Open 24 hours. Connects directly to the Cotai/Taipa area. Take Bus 25B to CTM (氹仔電訊).

By Sea

High-speed ferries depart regularly from Hong Kong (Sheung Wan or Tsim Sha Tsui) to Macau.

Macau Terminals

  • Taipa Ferry Terminal (氹仔): Closest to the Airport, Cotai, and CityU. Take Bus 26 to Est. Alm. M. Correia (高勵雅馬路) or a 6-minute taxi ride.
  • Outer Harbour Ferry Terminal (半島): Best if you are staying at a hotel on the Macau Peninsula before coming to campus.
